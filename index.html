Why I'm Running

Running 13.1 miles to honor Uncle Edwy's memory

There was a time when running even a mile felt out of reach. For years, I carried extra weight and the limits that came with it — limits I had quietly accepted. Over the past two years, I’ve lost more than 100 pounds and found something bigger than fitness. I found freedom. I learned that every barrier I thought was permanent could be moved with enough patience, effort, and belief.

Running a half marathon isn’t just about crossing a finish line. It’s about proving to myself that anything is possible. And it’s about using that belief to make a difference.

In April 2026, I’ll be running the Brooklyn Experience Half Marathon in honor of my Uncle Edwy, who we lost to colorectal cancer in 2021. Every mile is a tribute to him and to every family touched by this disease. Through this race, I’m raising money for St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center , two organizations that bring hope, healing, and life-saving care to those who need it most.

This run is my way of breaking past limits — both mine and the ones that cancer imposes on too many lives.

In Loving Memory

Edwy Bruce Aiken

Forever in Our Hearts | October 22, 1957 - February 26, 2021

❤️

Uncle Edwy was more than family. He was laughter, joy, and the heart of every gathering. When colorectal cancer took him from us in 2021 at 63 years old, our family lost more than an uncle; we lost our biggest supporter and the person who brought everyone together.

He had so much more life to live and so much more love to give. His story isn’t unique, and that’s what makes it so painful. So many families have faced the same loss. Remembering him has become more than holding onto the past. It’s a reminder to act, to fight for a cure, and to help others who are still battling this disease.

Honoring Uncle Edwy's Memory

Miles for Uncle Edwy is more than a race — it’s a promise. A promise to keep his memory alive, to honor his strength, and to help others who need hope.

Every mile matters. Every donation counts. Thank you for being part of this journey and helping make a difference in his name. 💙

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I donate?

You can donate directly by clicking the donation buttons above. Choose to support St. Jude Children's Hospital, Memorial Sloan Kettering, or both. All donations are processed securely through the respective organizations.

When is the race?

The Brooklyn Experience Half Marathon is scheduled for April 26, 2026 in Brooklyn, New York.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes! Both St. Jude Children's Research Hospital and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center are 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations, so your donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

Can my company match my donation?

Very likely! Many employers match charitable gifts made by employees (and sometimes spouses or retirees). To check:

1) Visit your company’s giving portal (e.g., Benevity, YourCause, CyberGrants) or ask HR/Benefits.
2) Search for St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital or Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and follow your company’s match instructions.
3) Make your donation using the links on this site, then submit your employer’s match request referencing your gift.

Matching gifts can double (or even triple) your impact for St. Jude and MSK. Thank you!

How can I let you know if my company is matching my donation?

That’s amazing—thank you for going the extra mile! After you submit your corporate match request, please let me know so I can track it and properly thank you:

• Forward your confirmation email (or a quick note with your name, company, and which org you matched—St. Jude or MSK) to info@milesforuncleedwy.org.
• Or include those details in the site’s contact form with the subject line Corporate Match.

This helps keep our totals accurate and ensures your generosity is recognized. 💛

How will the funds be used?

100% of donations go directly to the organizations you select. St. Jude uses funds for childhood cancer research and treatment. Memorial Sloan Kettering directs funds to colorectal cancer research and patient care.

Can I share this fundraiser?

Absolutely! Please use the social sharing buttons at the top of the page to spread the word. Every share helps us reach more supporters and get closer to our goal.
